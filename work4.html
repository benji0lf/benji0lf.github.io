Emergency Services Dispatcher II - Fresno Police

July 27, 2015-Present

From Sonitrol I got hired on at the Fresno Police Department as an Emergency Services Dispatcher. Here is a quick rundown of the 911 system in California: If you call 911 from a landline telephone the call will first be connected with the law enforcement agency that has jurisdiction over where you are calling from. The law enforcement dispatcher will screen the call to determine if police is needed or a different agency. If a different agency is needed (ex. Fire or medical) they then transfer to the agency that has jurisdiction over the emergency call. Similar, if you call from a cell phone you are connected with the closest law enforcement agency that your cell phone pings off of. Depending on your cell service and location you may or may not be connected with the right law enforcement agency. If this happens, you are transferred to the appropriate agency. This was new to me having come from a secondary 911 center (American Ambulance) where we would have all of our 911 calls transferred from a law enforcement agency. The police department deals with a lot of 911 pocket dials as well as children playing on phones. For me, dealing with and hearing 911 emergencies with crimes in progress is a lot more stressful than dealing with 911 medical emergencies. This job has helped me to conquer and regulate my stress levels when something critical is happening both at work and during my time away from work. At this job I have helped officers during vehicle pursuits that lasted all the way until Tulare county, I have talked to many victims of horrendous crimes, and have gotten to know certain constant callers by name & phone number. It is definitely not a job for everyone and I have learned a whole lot while working here both about myself and about criminal justice.
