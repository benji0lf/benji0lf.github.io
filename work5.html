Managing Stress

Stress is a huge part of my life considering the situations I deal with on a daily basis. Several years ago, I participated in a Critical Incident Stress Management course that taught me a lot about stress in the line of work that I am in. I am part of the Fresno Police Companion Officer program that serves as a peer support program within our department that will contact employees that have been involved in critical and high stress incidents to make sure talk about their experiences, vent any emotions they may be feeling, and learn about signs of stress and how to deal with them. Throughout the years I have learned that stress in response to an abnormal human experience can creep up days and even weeks after once the particular person is able to process what actually happened and this is normal. During the course of a stressful incident people may experience shock while others are taken over by their adrenaline and crash immediately after relieving themselves from the situation.
